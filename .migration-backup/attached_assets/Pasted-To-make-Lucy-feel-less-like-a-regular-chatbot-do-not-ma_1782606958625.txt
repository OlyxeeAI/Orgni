To make Lucy feel less like a regular chatbot, do **not** make the UI only a message box. Make Lucy feel like an **operations analyst inside Orgni**.

The key change:

> Lucy should not just “reply.” Lucy should inspect, explain, show evidence, suggest actions, and update the operating model.

## 1. Change Lucy from chat bubbles to “analysis cards”

Instead of only this:

> User asks question
> Lucy replies in one long paragraph

Use structured answer cards:

### Example UI

**Question:** “How do we onboard a supplier?”

Lucy response:

**Answer**
Your supplier onboarding process appears to have 5 steps.

**Workflow found**

1. Supplier submits company documents
2. Compliance checks registration and B-BBEE certificate
3. Finance verifies banking details
4. Manager approves supplier
5. Supplier is activated

**Evidence**

* Supplier Policy.pdf, page 3
* Procurement SOP.docx, section 2.1

**Confidence**
82%

**Missing / unclear**

* No document clearly says who gives final approval.

**Suggested action**
Create workflow: Supplier Onboarding
Create exception: Final approver unclear

That feels more human because Lucy is acting like an analyst, not a text generator.

## 2. Give Lucy “modes” instead of one generic chat

Add quick buttons above the input:

* **Ask**
* **Explain**
* **Find evidence**
* **Summarize**
* **Create workflow**
* **Check risk**
* **Find missing info**

So the user feels like Lucy has business skills.

Example:

User uploads documents. Lucy says:

> I found 3 possible workflows and 6 rules that need review.

Buttons:

* Review findings
* Show workflows
* Ask Lucy
* Find gaps

## 3. Make Lucy speak with business discipline

Lucy should use this pattern:

1. **Direct answer**
2. **Why I think this**
3. **Evidence**
4. **What is uncertain**
5. **Next action**

Example:

> Based on the uploaded documents, refunds require manager approval. I found this in the Refund Policy, page 4. I cannot confirm the approval time limit because no document mentions it. You can either upload the missing policy or create an exception.

This feels more trustworthy than:

> Sure, here is your refund process...

## 4. Add “I found this” behavior

After uploads, Lucy should proactively summarize like a human analyst.

Example:

> I finished reading the documents. I found:
> 4 workflows, 11 business rules, 3 approval steps, and 2 risks.
> 5 items need review before they become trusted knowledge.

Then show cards:

* Workflows detected
* Rules found
* Risks found
* Missing information
* Review needed

This makes Lucy feel alive without being fake.

## 5. Use side panels, not only chat

When Lucy answers, show supporting panels beside the chat:

### Left or center

Lucy’s explanation.

### Right side

* Sources used
* Confidence
* Related workflow
* Related rules
* Suggested actions

Example:

**Lucy answer**
“Finance must verify supplier banking details before approval.”

**Right panel**

* Source: Supplier Onboarding SOP, page 5
* Confidence: 91%
* Related workflow: Supplier Onboarding
* Related rule: Banking verification required
* Action: Approve rule / Edit rule / Add to workflow

This makes Orgni feel like software, not ChatGPT.

## 6. Let Lucy create objects, not just text

Every Lucy answer should be able to become something inside Orgni:

* Create knowledge item
* Create workflow
* Create exception
* Create rule
* Mark as approved
* Assign for review
* Export as JSON

Example:

Lucy says:

> I found an approval gap in supplier onboarding.

Buttons:

* Create exception
* Add approval step
* Ignore
* Ask follow-up

That is the difference between assistant and operating system.

## 7. Add “thinking trail,” not chain of thought

Do not show hidden reasoning. Show a business audit trail.

Example:

**How Lucy reached this**

* Searched 8 documents
* Used 3 relevant sections
* Ignored 2 unrelated results
* Found 1 conflict
* Confidence: 78%

This feels transparent without exposing internal reasoning.

## 8. Make empty states human

Instead of:

> No data found.

Use:

> I do not have enough business context yet. Upload an SOP, policy, invoice, spreadsheet, or process document, then I can map workflows and rules.

Instead of:

> I cannot answer.

Use:

> I cannot confirm this from your current sources. I can create a missing-information exception so your team knows what to upload or clarify.

## 9. UI structure for Lucy

Recommended Lucy screen:

### Top

Business context status:

* Sources indexed: 14
* Knowledge confidence: 76%
* Review needed: 8
* Open exceptions: 3

### Main area

Lucy conversation, but with structured cards.

### Right panel

Evidence and actions.

### Bottom input

Prompt box with action shortcuts:

* Ask about process
* Find rule
* Create workflow
* Check missing info
* Summarize business

## 10. Lucy personality

Lucy should not sound like a friend or a normal chatbot.

She should sound like a calm operations analyst:

* clear
* grounded
* no hype
* no guessing
* explains uncertainty
* always gives next action

Example tone:

> I can confirm this from two documents. The rule appears consistent, but the approver role is unclear. I recommend marking this workflow as review_needed until the approver is confirmed.

## 11. Best product principle

Do not make Lucy feel human by adding fake emotion.

Make Lucy feel human by making her **useful like a human analyst**:

* remembers context
* organizes information
* explains clearly
* shows evidence
* admits uncertainty
* asks for missing info
* turns answers into action

That is how Lucy becomes different from a regular chatbot.
